The development of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection is a rapidly evolving field. According to (Dwivedi et al., 2023), generative conversational AI has the potential to transform research, practice, and policy, but it also poses significant challenges. One of the key opportunities of AI in academia is its ability to assist with peer review, as noted by (Meyer et al., 2023). However, the use of AI in peer review also raises concerns about academic integrity, as highlighted by (Cotton et al., 2023).

The application of machine learning techniques in various fields, including fluid mechanics (Brunton et al., 2019) and cyber security (Shaukat et al., 2020), demonstrates the potential of AI to improve validation and detection processes. Moreover, the use of large language models, as reviewed by (Raiaan et al., 2024), can facilitate the development of autonomous self-correcting AI agents.

In the context of academic citation fraud detection, AI can play a crucial role in identifying and preventing fraudulent activities. As (Dwivedi et al., 2023) suggest, AI can help to detect plagiarism and other forms of academic misconduct. Furthermore, the use of AI in education can also help to promote critical thinking and academic integrity, as noted by (Naqvi et al., 2025) and (Chan et al., 2023).

In conclusion, the development of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection is a complex and multifaceted issue. While AI has the potential to transform academia, it also poses significant challenges that need to be addressed. Further research is needed to fully explore the opportunities and challenges of AI in academia.
