The integration of Agentic AI in large language models has been a topic of interest in recent literature, particularly in the context of reliable academic literature review and hallucination mitigation. According to GPT-4 Technical Report (2023) by OpenAI, Wijayagunawardhana, W. A. Hasindu N., large language models have shown promising results in generating human-like text, but are also prone to hallucinations (OpenAI, 2023). This is echoed by Zhang and Li (2023), who conducted a survey on hallucination in large language models, highlighting the need for mitigation strategies (Zhang & Li, 2023).

In the context of education, several papers have explored the potential of AI and ChatGPT in educational settings. Rudolph and Tan (2023) argue that ChatGPT has the potential to revolutionize traditional assessments in higher education, while Grassini (2023) and Rasul and Nair (2023) discuss the benefits and challenges of integrating ChatGPT in educational settings (Rudolph & Tan, 2023; Grassini, 2023; Rasul & Nair, 2023). However, Stahl and Eke (2023) raise ethical concerns regarding the use of ChatGPT, highlighting the need for responsible AI development (Stahl & Eke, 2023).

In terms of applications, large language models have been explored in various fields, including healthcare (Alowais & Alghamdi, 2023; Tian & Jin, 2023) and computational social science (Ziems & Held, 2023). However, Hadi and Al Tashi (2023) identify limitations and challenges associated with the practical usage of large language models, including hallucinations and lack of transparency (Hadi & Al Tashi, 2023). Feuerriegel and Hartmann (2023) and Banh and Strobel (2023) provide an overview of generative AI, highlighting its potential and challenges (Feuerriegel & Hartmann, 2023; Banh & Strobel, 2023).
