The development of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection is a rapidly evolving field. According to (Dwivedi et al., 2023), the emergence of generative conversational AI, such as ChatGPT, has significant implications for research, practice, and policy. However, this also raises concerns about academic integrity, as highlighted by (Cotton et al., 2023) and (Kővári, 2025), who emphasize the need for best practices to combat AI-induced plagiarism.

The application of machine learning techniques in various fields, including cyber security (Shaukat et al., 2020) and disease diagnosis (Ahsan et al., 2022), demonstrates the potential of AI in improving accuracy and efficiency. Similarly, the use of large language models, as reviewed by (Raiaan et al., 2024), can be leveraged for peer review validation and citation fraud detection.

In the context of education, (Chen et al., 2020) provide a comprehensive review of artificial intelligence applications, while (Zawacki‐Richter et al., 2019) highlight the need for educator involvement in AI development. The emergence of deepfake technology, as discussed by (Westerlund, 2019), also has implications for academic integrity and the need for autonomous self-correcting AI agents.

The development of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection requires a multidisciplinary approach, incorporating insights from education, computer science, and social sciences. As (Rudolph et al., 2023) suggest, the use of ChatGPT and similar technologies may signal the end of traditional assessments in higher education, emphasizing the need for innovative solutions to ensure academic integrity.
