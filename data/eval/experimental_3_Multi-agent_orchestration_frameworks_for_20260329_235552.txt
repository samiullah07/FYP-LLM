The concept of multi-agent orchestration frameworks for knowledge-intensive reasoning and automated literature synthesis is a rapidly evolving field. According to (Lei Wang, Chen Ma, Xueyang Feng, 2024), large language models have been increasingly used to develop autonomous agents. These agents can be integrated into multi-agent systems to facilitate knowledge-intensive reasoning (Andy Clark, 2013). 

The use of deep learning techniques has also been explored in this context, with (Iqbal H. Sarker, 2021) providing a comprehensive overview of deep learning techniques, taxonomy, applications, and research directions. Furthermore, (Peter Kairouz, H. Brendan McMahan, Brendan Avent, 2020) discuss the advances and open problems in federated learning, which can be applied to multi-agent orchestration frameworks.

In addition, the concept of digital transformation (Peter C. Verhoef, Thijs Broekhuizen, Yakov Bart, 2019) and digital platform ecosystems (Andreas Hein, Maximilian Schreieck, Tobias Riasanow, 2019) can provide insights into the development of multi-agent orchestration frameworks. The use of edge computing and edge intelligence (Zhi Zhou, Xu Chen, En Li, 2019) can also enhance the performance of these frameworks.

However, the development of multi-agent orchestration frameworks for knowledge-intensive reasoning and automated literature synthesis is still in its early stages, and more research is needed to fully explore its potential. As (Daniele Miorandi, Sabrina Sicari, Francesco De Pellegrini, 2012) note, the internet of things and related technologies can provide a foundation for the development of these frameworks.
