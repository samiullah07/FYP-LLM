The concept of multi-agent orchestration frameworks for knowledge-intensive reasoning and automated literature synthesis has garnered significant attention in recent years. In the realm of artificial intelligence, researchers have explored its applications in various domains, including supply chain management (Reza Toorajipour and Vahid Sohrabpour, 2020) and business value creation (Ida Merete Enholm and Emmanouil Papagiannidis, 2021). The integration of edge computing with artificial intelligence has also been investigated, with a focus on enhancing edge intelligence (Zhi Zhou and Xu Chen, 2019).

The use of large language models has been surveyed in the context of autonomous agents (Lei Wang and Chen Ma, 2024) and retrieval-augmented generation (Yunfan Gao and Yun Xiong, 2023). These studies highlight the potential of artificial intelligence in automating literature synthesis and knowledge-intensive reasoning. However, there is a need for further research on the capabilities required for humans to effectively interact with artificial intelligence systems (Lina Markauskaitė and Rebecca Marrone, 2022).

In contrast, some studies have focused on the applications of artificial intelligence in specific domains, such as medical imaging informatics (Andreas S. Panayides and Amir A. Amini, 2020) and education (Petros Lameras and Sylvester Arnab, 2021). The use of service-oriented middleware has also been explored in the context of the Future Internet (Valérie Issarny and Nikolaos Georgantas, 2011). While these studies provide valuable insights, there is a gap in the literature regarding the development of multi-agent orchestration frameworks for knowledge-intensive reasoning and automated literature synthesis.
