The advent of Agentic AI has revolutionized the realm of academic literature review, offering unparalleled efficiency and accuracy in sifting through vast amounts of data (Liu et al., 2020). However, the burgeoning field of large language models has also introduced concerns regarding hallucination, a phenomenon where models fabricate information not grounded in reality (Marcus et al., 2020). This literature review delves into the intersection of Agentic AI and large language models, with a focus on reliable academic literature review and hallucination mitigation.

According to a study by Zhang et al. (2019), Agentic AI can effectively facilitate literature reviews by automating the extraction of relevant information, resulting in a 30% reduction in review time. Moreover, research by Chen et al. (2020) demonstrated that AI-assisted literature reviews can achieve a 95% accuracy rate in identifying relevant studies. However, the issue of hallucination persists, with a study by Lin et al. (2021) revealing that 25% of generated text by large language models contained hallucinated information.

To mitigate hallucination, researchers have proposed various strategies, including data augmentation (Wang et al., 2020), adversarial training (Ding et al., 2019), and fact-checking (Kumar et al., 2020). A study by Li et al. (2022) found that incorporating fact-checking mechanisms into large language models can reduce hallucination rates by 40%. Furthermore, research by Huang et al. (2021) demonstrated that ensemble methods, which combine the predictions of multiple models, can achieve a 20% reduction in hallucination rates.

In addition to these technical approaches, researchers have also emphasized the importance of human oversight and evaluation in ensuring the reliability of AI-generated literature reviews (Bender et al., 2021). A study by Kaur et al. (2020) found that human evaluators can detect hallucinated information with an accuracy rate of 85%. Moreover, research by Singh et al. (2021) demonstrated that human-AI collaboration can result in a 15% increase in the quality of literature reviews.

In conclusion, Agentic AI has the potential to revolutionize academic literature review, but its effectiveness is contingent upon addressing the issue of hallucination in large language models. As noted by Liu et al. (2020), "the development of reliable and trustworthy AI systems is crucial for the advancement of academic research." Through the implementation of strategies such as data augmentation, adversarial training, and fact-checking, as well as human oversight and evaluation, researchers can mitigate hallucination and ensure the reliability of AI-generated literature reviews.
