The integration of Agentic AI in academic literature review has the potential to enhance the reliability and efficiency of the process (Kasneci et al., 2023). However, one of the significant challenges associated with large language models is hallucination, which can lead to inaccurate information (Dwivedi et al., 2023). Hallucination in AI refers to the phenomenon where the model generates false or misleading information, which can have severe consequences in academic research (Dwivedi et al., 2023).

To mitigate hallucination, explainable artificial intelligence (XAI) can be employed, which provides insights into the decision-making process of the model (Barredo Arrieta et al., 2019). XAI can help identify potential biases and errors in the model, enabling researchers to take corrective measures. Furthermore, the use of XAI can promote responsible AI practices, which is essential for maintaining the integrity of academic research (Barredo Arrieta et al., 2019).

The application of Agentic AI in academic literature review can also facilitate the analysis of large volumes of data, enabling researchers to identify patterns and trends that may not be apparent through traditional methods (Zawacki-Richter et al., 2019). However, it is crucial to ensure that the AI models used are transparent, accountable, and free from biases (Dwivedi et al., 2019).

In conclusion, the integration of Agentic AI in academic literature review has the potential to enhance the reliability and efficiency of the process. However, it is essential to address the challenge of hallucination through the use of XAI and other mitigation strategies. By promoting responsible AI practices, researchers can ensure the integrity and validity of their findings.
