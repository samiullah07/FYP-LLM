The integration of Agentic AI in large language models has been a topic of interest in recent literature, particularly in the context of reliable academic literature review and hallucination mitigation. According to GPT-4 Technical Report (2023) by OpenAI, Wijayagunawardhana, W. A. Hasindu N., large language models have shown significant potential in generating human-like text, but are also prone to hallucinations (OpenAI, 2023). This is further supported by Zhang and Li (2023), who conducted a survey on hallucination in large language models, highlighting the need for mitigation strategies (Zhang & Li, 2023).

In the context of education, several authors have explored the potential of ChatGPT and large language models. Rudolph and Tan (2023) argue that ChatGPT has the potential to revolutionize traditional assessments in higher education, while Tareq Rasul and Sumesh Nair (2023) discuss the benefits and challenges of using ChatGPT in higher education (Rudolph & Tan, 2023; Rasul & Nair, 2023). Similarly, Grassini (2023) and Roumeliotis and Tselikas (2023) explore the potential and consequences of AI and ChatGPT in educational settings, highlighting the need for careful consideration of the limitations and challenges (Grassini, 2023; Roumeliotis & Tselikas, 2023).

In contrast, some authors have raised concerns about the ethics of ChatGPT and large language models. Stahl and Eke (2023) explore the ethical issues surrounding ChatGPT, highlighting the need for responsible development and deployment (Stahl & Eke, 2023). Furthermore, Hadi and Al Tashi (2023) discuss the limitations and challenges of large language models, including hallucination and bias (Hadi & Al Tashi, 2023).

Overall, the literature suggests that while large language models have significant potential, they also require careful consideration of their limitations and challenges. Further research is needed to develop effective strategies for mitigating hallucinations and ensuring the reliable use of Agentic AI in academic literature review.
