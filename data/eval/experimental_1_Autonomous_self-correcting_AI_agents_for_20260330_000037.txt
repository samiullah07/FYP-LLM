The development of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection is a rapidly evolving field. According to Yogesh K. Dwivedi et al. However, this also raises concerns about academic integrity, as highlighted by Debby Cotton et al. (2023). The use of AI in academia can facilitate cheating, and it is essential to ensure the authenticity of academic work.

The application of machine learning techniques can help detect citation fraud and validate peer reviews (Kamran Shaukat et al., 2020). Moreover, large language models, such as those reviewed by Mohaimenul Azam Khan Raiaan et al. (2024), can be utilized to analyze and identify patterns in academic writing. Jesse G. Meyer et al. (2023) also discuss the opportunities and challenges of using large language models in academia, including their potential to assist in peer review validation.

The importance of academic integrity in the era of ChatGPT is emphasized by Debby Cotton et al. (2023), who suggest that educators must be aware of the potential risks and take measures to prevent cheating. Yogesh K. Dwivedi et al. (2023) also highlight the need for multidisciplinary perspectives on the opportunities, challenges, and implications of generative conversational AI for research, practice, and policy.

In conclusion, the development of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection is a complex issue that requires careful consideration of the opportunities and challenges presented by AI technology. Further research is needed to fully explore the potential of AI in academia and to ensure the integrity of academic work.
