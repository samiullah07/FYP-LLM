The integration of large language models in automated academic peer review and citation generation has sparked intense debate regarding its ethical implications. According to Kasneci et al. (2023), large language models such as ChatGPT present both opportunities and challenges for education, highlighting the need for careful consideration of their potential impact. Dwivedi et al. (2023) further emphasize the importance of examining the multidisciplinary perspectives on the opportunities, challenges, and implications of generative conversational AI for research, practice, and policy.

The use of artificial intelligence in achieving the Sustainable Development Goals has been explored by Vinuesa et al. (2020), who highlight the potential benefits and challenges of AI in this context. However, the specific application of large language models in academic peer review and citation generation raises concerns regarding academic integrity, as noted by Cotton et al. (2023). The potential for ChatGPT to generate high-quality content has led to concerns about cheating and the need for new assessment strategies, as discussed by Rudolph et al. (2023) and Jürgen et al. (2023).

The ethics of using large language models in scholarly publishing have been examined by Brady et al. (2023), who highlight the need for transparency and accountability in the use of AI-generated content. Furthermore, the systematic review of research on artificial intelligence applications in higher education by Zawacki‐Richter et al. (2019) emphasizes the importance of considering the role of educators in the development and implementation of AI-based systems.

In conclusion, the integration of large language models in automated academic peer review and citation generation raises significant ethical concerns that need to be addressed. As highlighted by the literature, it is essential to consider the potential benefits and challenges of these models and to develop strategies for ensuring academic integrity and transparency in their use.
