The integration of Agentic AI in academic literature reviews has the potential to revolutionize the way researchers conduct and analyze studies. According to Zhao and Zhou (2023), large language models have made significant progress in recent years, enabling them to process and generate vast amounts of text. This capability can be leveraged to automate the literature review process, reducing the time and effort required to identify and synthesize relevant studies (Rudolph and Tan, 2023). However, concerns have been raised regarding the potential for hallucination, where AI models generate false or inaccurate information (Feuerriegel and Hartmann, 2023).

To mitigate this risk, researchers have emphasized the importance of developing and implementing robust evaluation frameworks to assess the reliability and accuracy of Agentic AI-generated content (Stahl and Eke, 2023). Furthermore, studies have highlighted the need for transparency and explainability in AI decision-making processes to ensure that users can understand and trust the output (Banh and Strobel, 2023). In the context of academic literature reviews, this may involve integrating human oversight and review processes to verify the accuracy and validity of AI-generated content (Rasul and Nair, 2023).

Recent surveys and reviews have also explored the applications and limitations of large language models in various fields, including education (Grassini, 2023), healthcare (Alowais and Alghamdi, 2023), and social science (Ziems and Held, 2023). These studies demonstrate the vast potential of Agentic AI to transform research practices and knowledge dissemination. Nevertheless, it is essential to address the challenges and limitations associated with AI-generated content to ensure the reliability and validity of academic literature reviews.
