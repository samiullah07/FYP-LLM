The concept of retrieval-augmented generation has gained significant attention in recent years, particularly in the context of reducing hallucinations in large language models (LLMs). Hallucinations in LLMs refer to the generation of inaccurate or nonsensical information, which can have severe consequences in various applications (S. M Towhidul Islam Tonmoy, S M Mehedi Zaman, Vinija Jain et al., 2024). To mitigate this issue, researchers have explored the use of retrieval-augmented generation techniques, which involve combining the strengths of retrieval-based and generation-based approaches (Wan Zhang, Jing Zhang, 2025).

Studies have shown that retrieval-augmentation can significantly reduce hallucinations in LLMs, particularly in conversational settings (Kurt Shuster, Spencer Poff, Moya Chen et al., 2021). For instance, a systematic survey on retrieval-augmented generation for educational applications highlighted the potential of this approach in reducing hallucinations and improving the overall performance of LLMs (Zongxi Li, Zijian Wang, Weiming Wang et al., 2025). Similarly, a review of hallucination mitigation techniques in LLMs emphasized the importance of retrieval-augmentation in reducing hallucinations and improving the reliability of LLMs (S. M Towhidul Islam Tonmoy, S M Mehedi Zaman, Vinija Jain et al., 2024).

While there is a growing body of research on retrieval-augmented generation, there are still significant gaps in our understanding of this approach. For example, the application of retrieval-augmented generation in medical education and healthcare administration is still in its infancy, and more research is needed to fully explore its potential (Josip Vrdoljak, Zvonimir Boban, Marino Vilović et al., 2025). Additionally, the use of LLMs in other domains, such as cybersecurity and metaverse applications, is still a relatively underexplored area (Muhammad Mudassar Yamin, Ehtesham Hashmi, Mohib Ullah et al., 2024; Sangmin Park, Young‐Gab Kim, 2022).

In conclusion, retrieval-augmented generation has shown significant promise in reducing hallucinations in LLMs, and further research is needed to fully explore its potential. By building on the existing body of research, we can work towards developing more reliable and accurate LLMs that can be applied in a wide range of domains.
