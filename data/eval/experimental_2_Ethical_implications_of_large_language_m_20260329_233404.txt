The integration of large language models in automated academic peer review and citation generation has sparked intense debate regarding its ethical implications. According to Kasneci et al. (2023), large language models, such as ChatGPT, present both opportunities and challenges for education. One of the primary concerns is the potential for these models to generate artificial intelligence-written research papers, raising questions about authorship and authenticity (Lund et al., 2023). 

Dwivedi et al. (2023) highlight the need for multidisciplinary perspectives on the opportunities, challenges, and implications of generative conversational AI for research, practice, and policy. The use of large language models in academic settings also raises concerns about academic integrity, with some arguing that it could facilitate cheating (Cotton et al., 2023). On the other hand, Singhal et al. (2023) demonstrate that large language models can encode clinical knowledge, suggesting potential benefits for certain fields of research.

The role of artificial intelligence in achieving the Sustainable Development Goals is also relevant to this discussion, as highlighted by Vinuesa et al. (2020). However, the use of large language models in academic settings requires careful consideration of the ethical implications, including issues related to authorship, authenticity, and academic integrity (Dwivedi et al., 2019). 

As the academic community continues to grapple with the implications of large language models, it is essential to consider the potential consequences for scholarly publishing and the future of education (Grassini, 2023). Ultimately, the integration of large language models in automated academic peer review and citation generation requires a nuanced understanding of the ethical implications and a commitment to ensuring academic integrity.
