The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) in various fields has sparked a plethora of research, with a significant focus on its applications and implications. In the context of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection, several papers have explored the potential of AI in research and practice (Dwivedi and Kshetri, 2023; Resnik and Hosseini, 2024). For instance, Dwivedi and Kshetri (2023) discuss the opportunities and challenges of generative conversational AI in research, while Resnik and Hosseini (2024) highlight the need for new guidance on the ethics of using AI in scientific research.

The use of AI in environmental sustainability and healthcare has also been explored, with papers discussing the potential of AI-driven solutions for eco-cities (Bibri and Krogstie, 2023) and the challenges of balancing privacy and progress in AI-driven healthcare (Williamson and Prybutok, 2024). Furthermore, the application of AI in digital healthcare has been examined, with Wang and Su (2021) discussing the key technologies, applications, and challenges of digital healthcare in the context of the Covid-19 pandemic.

In terms of the technical aspects of AI, papers have investigated the impact of word embedding models on text analytics (Asudani and Nagwani, 2023) and the applications of explainable AI (XAI) (Saarela and Podgorelec, 2024; De Bock and Coussement, 2023). However, there is a gap in the literature regarding the specific application of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection. While papers have discussed the potential of AI in research and practice, there is a need for further research on the development and implementation of AI agents for peer review validation and citation fraud detection.
