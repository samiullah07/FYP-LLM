The integration of Agentic AI in academic literature review has the potential to enhance the reliability and accuracy of research findings (Kasneci et al., 2023). However, the use of large language models also raises concerns about hallucination, which can lead to the generation of false or misleading information (Dwivedi et al., 2023). To mitigate this risk, it is essential to develop explainable artificial intelligence (XAI) models that can provide transparent and interpretable results (Barredo Arrieta et al., 2019).

The application of AI in higher education has been explored in various studies, with a focus on the opportunities and challenges associated with its adoption (Zawacki-Richter et al., 2019). The use of ChatGPT, a large language model, has been found to have both positive and negative implications for education, including the potential to enhance student learning outcomes and the risk of promoting academic dishonesty (Kasneci et al., 2023; Rudolph et al., 2023).

To ensure the responsible use of AI in academic research, it is crucial to develop a multidisciplinary approach that incorporates perspectives from various fields, including education, computer science, and social sciences (Dwivedi et al., 2019). The development of agentic AI models that can interact with humans in a transparent and accountable manner is also essential for building trust in AI-generated research findings (Feuerriegel et al., 2023).

In conclusion, the use of Agentic AI in academic literature review has the potential to enhance the reliability and accuracy of research findings, but it also raises concerns about hallucination and the need for explainable AI models. Further research is needed to develop a multidisciplinary approach to AI adoption in academia and to ensure the responsible use of large language models in research.
