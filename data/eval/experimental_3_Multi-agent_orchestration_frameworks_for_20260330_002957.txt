The concept of multi-agent orchestration frameworks for knowledge-intensive reasoning and automated literature synthesis is a complex and multidisciplinary field. According to Barredo Arrieta et al. (2019), explainable artificial intelligence (XAI) is a crucial aspect of this field, as it enables the development of transparent and accountable AI systems. The authors highlight the importance of XAI in various applications, including those that require knowledge-intensive reasoning.

In the context of knowledge creation, Bathelt et al. (2004) discuss the role of clusters and local buzz in facilitating the process of knowledge creation. The authors argue that global pipelines and local buzz are essential for the creation of new knowledge, which is a critical component of multi-agent orchestration frameworks. Furthermore, Dwivedi et al. (2019) provide a comprehensive overview of the opportunities and challenges associated with artificial intelligence, including its applications in knowledge-intensive reasoning.

The use of artificial intelligence in achieving sustainable development goals is also relevant to this field, as highlighted by Vinuesa et al. (2020). The authors discuss the potential of AI in addressing various sustainable development goals, including those related to education and healthcare. In addition, the application of machine learning in materials science is another area of research that is relevant to multi-agent orchestration frameworks, as discussed by Batra et al. (2020).

The development of digital business strategies is also crucial for the successful implementation of multi-agent orchestration frameworks, as highlighted by Bharadwaj et al. (2013). The authors discuss the importance of digital innovation management in reinventing innovation management research in a digital world. Moreover, the concept of edge intelligence, as discussed by Zhou et al. (2019), is also relevant to this field, as it enables the development of intelligent systems that can operate at the edge of the network.
