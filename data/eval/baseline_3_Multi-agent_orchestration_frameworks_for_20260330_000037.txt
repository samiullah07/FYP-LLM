The concept of multi-agent orchestration frameworks for knowledge-intensive reasoning and automated literature synthesis has gained significant attention in recent years. In the context of artificial intelligence, Reza Toorajipour and Vahid Sohrabpour (2020) highlight the importance of AI in supply chain management, while Lei Wang and Chen Ma (2024) discuss the role of large language models in autonomous agents. Similarly, Yunfan Gao and Yun Xiong (2023) explore the potential of retrieval-augmented generation for large language models.

In terms of technological disruptions, Dimitrios Buhalis and Tracy Harwood (2019) examine the impact of AI on services, particularly in the tourism and hospitality sector. Meanwhile, Rohit Batra and Le Song (2020) discuss the application of machine learning in emerging materials intelligence ecosystems. The use of AI in medical imaging informatics is also explored by Andreas S. Panayides and Amir A. Amini (2020).

The integration of AI with human learning is another significant area of research. Petros Lameras and Sylvester Arnab (2021) investigate the role of AI in education, while Lina Markauskaitė and Rebecca Marrone (2022) rethink the entwinement between AI and human learning. In contrast, Ilya Jackson and Dmitry Ivanov (2024) focus on the application of generative AI in supply chain and operations management.

There is a notable gap in the literature regarding the application of multi-agent orchestration frameworks in knowledge-intensive reasoning and automated literature synthesis. While Ángel Lagares Lemos and Florian Daniel (2015) discuss web service composition, and Salman Bahoo and Marco Cucculelli (2022) explore AI and corporate innovation, there is a need for further research in this area. Additionally, the papers by J.D. Fernandez and F. Vico (2013), and Valérie Issarny and Nikolaos Georgantas (2011) provide insights into AI methods in algorithmic composition and service-oriented middleware, respectively, but do not directly address the topic of multi-agent orchestration frameworks.
