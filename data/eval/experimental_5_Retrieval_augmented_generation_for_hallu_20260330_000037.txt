The integration of retrieval augmentation in large language models has been shown to be effective in reducing hallucination in their outputs (Wan Zhang et al., 2025). Hallucination refers to the generation of content that is not based on any actual input or context, and it is a significant problem in large language models (Kurt Shuster et al., 2021). Retrieval augmentation involves the use of external knowledge sources to inform the generation process, and it has been found to improve the accuracy and reliability of large language models (Gongbo Zhang et al., 2025).

The use of retrieval augmentation in large language models has been explored in various domains, including medical question answering (Gongbo Zhang et al., 2025) and legal research (Varun Magesh et al., 2025). In these domains, the ability of large language models to generate accurate and reliable outputs is critical, and retrieval augmentation has been shown to be an effective way to achieve this (Wan Zhang et al., 2025).

The effectiveness of retrieval augmentation in reducing hallucination in large language models is due to its ability to provide the models with access to external knowledge sources that can inform the generation process (Yanming Liu et al., 2024). This allows the models to generate outputs that are based on actual input or context, rather than on hallucinations (Kurt Shuster et al., 2021).

In conclusion, the use of retrieval augmentation in large language models is a promising approach for reducing hallucination in their outputs (Wan Zhang et al., 2025).
