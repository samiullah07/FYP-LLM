The integration of Agentic AI in academic literature reviews has the potential to revolutionize the way researchers conduct and synthesize research (GPT-4 Technical Report, 2023). Agentic AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that can perform tasks autonomously, making decisions and taking actions based on their programming and goals (Stefan Feuerriegel and Jochen Hartmann, 2023). In the context of academic literature reviews, Agentic AI can help mitigate the issue of hallucination, where AI models generate false or misleading information (Konstantinos I. Roumeliotis and Nikolaos D. Tselikas, 2023). 

Several studies have explored the applications and challenges of large language models, including their potential to transform computational social science (Caleb Ziems and William A. Held, 2023) and biomedicine (Shubo Tian and Qiao Jin, 2023). However, these models also pose significant challenges, including the risk of perpetuating biases and inaccuracies (Muhammad Usman Hadi and Qasem Al Tashi, 2023). To address these challenges, researchers have emphasized the need for transparency, accountability, and human oversight in the development and deployment of Agentic AI systems (Bernd Carsten Stahl and Damian Eke, 2023).

In educational settings, Agentic AI has the potential to support students and teachers in conducting literature reviews, providing personalized feedback, and promoting critical thinking (Simone Grassini, 2023). However, there are also concerns about the potential for Agentic AI to disrupt traditional assessment methods and exacerbate existing inequalities (Jürgen Rudolph and Samson Tan, 2023). 

To fully realize the potential of Agentic AI in academic literature reviews, further research is needed to address the challenges of hallucination mitigation, bias, and accountability. This includes the development of more sophisticated AI models that can accurately identify and correct errors, as well as the creation of frameworks for human-AI collaboration that prioritize transparency and trust (Lixiang Yan and Samuel Greiff, 2024).
