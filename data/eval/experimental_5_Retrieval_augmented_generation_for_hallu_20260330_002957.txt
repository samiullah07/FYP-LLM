The integration of retrieval augmentation in large language models has been shown to be effective in reducing hallucination in their outputs (Wan Zhang et al., 2025). Hallucination refers to the phenomenon where language models generate text that is not based on any actual input or context, leading to inaccuracies and potential misinformation. Retrieval augmentation involves the use of external knowledge sources to inform the generation process, thereby reducing the likelihood of hallucination (Kurt Shuster et al., 2021).

The application of retrieval augmentation in large language models has been explored in various domains, including medical education and clinical decision support (Josip Vrdoljak et al., 2025). In this context, the use of retrieval augmentation can help to improve the accuracy and reliability of language model outputs, which is critical in high-stakes applications such as healthcare (Varun Magesh et al., 2025).

The effectiveness of retrieval augmentation in reducing hallucination has been demonstrated in several studies. For example, Gongbo Zhang et al. (2025) showed that the use of retrieval augmentation in medical question answering can lead to significant improvements in performance. Similarly, Yanming Liu et al. (2024) demonstrated the effectiveness of retrieval augmentation in learning to answer and understand questions via iterative self-feedback.

The use of retrieval augmentation can also be combined with other techniques, such as prompting, to further improve the performance of large language models (Pengfei Liu et al., 2022). However, the potential implications of retrieval augmentation on the reliability and trustworthiness of language model outputs are still being explored (Yogesh K. Dwivedi et al., 2023).

In conclusion, the integration of retrieval augmentation in large language models has been shown to be effective in reducing hallucination and improving the accuracy and reliability of their outputs. Further research is needed to fully explore the potential of retrieval augmentation and its applications in various domains.
