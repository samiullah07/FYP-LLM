The concept of multi-agent orchestration frameworks for knowledge-intensive reasoning and automated literature synthesis is a complex and multidisciplinary field. According to (Barredo Arrieta et al., 2019), explainable artificial intelligence (XAI) is a crucial aspect of this field, as it enables the development of transparent and accountable AI systems. In the context of XAI, (Sarker, 2021) highlights the importance of deep learning techniques, which can be used to develop predictive models for various applications.

The use of large language models, such as those discussed in (OpenAI et al., 2023) and (Kasneci et al., 2023), can also be beneficial for automated literature synthesis. These models can be used to generate text and provide feedback, as noted in (Gao et al., 2023). However, the development of such models requires careful consideration of their potential applications and challenges, as discussed in (Clark, 2013).

In addition to XAI and large language models, other technologies, such as the Internet of Things (IoT), can also be used to support multi-agent orchestration frameworks. According to (Zanella et al., 2014) and (Miorandi et al., 2012), IoT can be used to develop smart systems that can interact with their environment and make decisions autonomously.

The development of multi-agent orchestration frameworks also requires the use of various tools and methods, such as those discussed in (Voinov et al., 2018). These tools and methods can be used to support participatory modeling and decision-making, which are critical aspects of knowledge-intensive reasoning.

In conclusion, the development of multi-agent orchestration frameworks for knowledge-intensive reasoning and automated literature synthesis is a complex task that requires the use of various technologies and techniques. By considering the concepts and challenges discussed in the literature, developers can create more effective and accountable systems.
