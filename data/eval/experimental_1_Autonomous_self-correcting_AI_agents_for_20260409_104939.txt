The development of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection has gained significant attention in recent years (Dwivedi et al., 2023; Cotton et al., 2023). The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) in higher education has been explored in various studies, highlighting its potential to enhance the learning experience (Olaf Zawacki‐Richter et al., 2019). However, the use of AI in academic settings also raises concerns about academic integrity, particularly with the emergence of generative conversational AI such as ChatGPT (Dwivedi et al., 2023; Meyer et al., 2023).

Research on machine learning techniques has shown its effectiveness in various applications, including fluid mechanics (Brunton et al., 2019), supply chain management (Toorajipour et al., 2020), and cyber security (Shaukat et al., 2020). The use of machine learning algorithms can also be applied to disease diagnosis (Ahsan et al., 2022) and large language models (Raiaan et al., 2024). However, the application of AI in academic settings requires careful consideration of its implications on academic integrity and the potential for citation fraud (Cotton et al., 2023; Dwivedi et al., 2023).

The emergence of large language models has raised concerns about the potential for academic misconduct, including plagiarism and citation fraud (Dwivedi et al., 2023; Meyer et al., 2023). Studies have highlighted the need for educators to be aware of the potential risks and benefits of using AI in academic settings (Olaf Zawacki‐Richter et al., 2019; Chan et al., 2023). Furthermore, research has shown that students' perceptions of generative AI vary, with some seeing it as a beneficial tool for learning, while others are concerned about its potential to facilitate academic misconduct (Chan et al., 2023).

The development of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection requires a multidisciplinary approach, incorporating insights from education, computer science, and social sciences (Dwivedi et al., 2023; Meyer et al., 2023). While there are potential benefits to using AI in academic settings, including enhanced efficiency and accuracy, there are also challenges and risks that need to be addressed (Cotton et al., 2023; Naqvi et al., 2025). Further research is needed to explore the implications of AI on academic integrity and to develop effective strategies for mitigating the risks associated with its use.

In conclusion, the development of autonomous self-correcting AI agents for peer review validation and academic citation fraud detection is a complex issue that requires careful consideration of its implications on academic integrity. While there are potential benefits to using AI in academic settings, there are also challenges and risks that need to be addressed (Dwivedi et al., 2023; Cotton et al., 2023). Further research is needed to explore the implications of AI on academic integrity and to develop effective strategies for mitigating the risks associated with its use.
