The use of large language models has been increasingly prevalent in various applications, including medical education and clinical decision support (Vrdoljak et al., 2025). However, one of the significant challenges associated with these models is hallucination, which refers to the generation of false or inaccurate information (Wan Zhang et al., 2025). Retrieval augmented generation has been proposed as a potential solution to mitigate hallucination in large language model outputs (Kurt Shuster et al., 2021).

Retrieval augmented language models have been shown to be effective in reducing hallucination, particularly in medical question answering tasks (Gongbo Zhang et al., 2025). These models leverage long context to generate more accurate and reliable responses. Additionally, the use of iterative self-feedback mechanisms, such as those proposed in RA-ISF, can further improve the performance of retrieval augmented language models (Yanming Liu et al., 2024).

The importance of mitigating hallucination in large language models cannot be overstated, particularly in high-stakes applications such as healthcare (Varun Magesh et al., 2025). The reliability of leading AI legal research tools has also been assessed, highlighting the need for more robust and accurate language models (Varun Magesh et al., 2025).

In conclusion, retrieval augmented generation has been shown to be an effective approach to reducing hallucination in large language model outputs. Further research is needed to fully explore the potential of these models and to develop more robust and reliable language models for various applications.
