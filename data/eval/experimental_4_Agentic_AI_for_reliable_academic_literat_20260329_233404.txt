The integration of Agentic AI in academic literature review has the potential to enhance the reliability and accuracy of research findings (Kasneci et al., 2023). However, the use of large language models also raises concerns about hallucination, which can lead to the generation of false or misleading information (Dwivedi et al., 2023). To mitigate this risk, it is essential to develop explainable artificial intelligence (XAI) models that can provide transparent and interpretable results (Barredo Arrieta et al., 2019).

The application of AI in higher education has been increasingly explored, with a focus on its potential to improve learning outcomes and enhance the educational experience (Zawacki‐Richter et al., 2019). However, the use of AI in academic settings also raises important questions about the role of educators and the potential for AI to replace human instructors (Zawacki‐Richter et al., 2019). Recent studies have investigated the use of ChatGPT in educational settings, highlighting both the opportunities and challenges associated with this technology (Kasneci et al., 2023; Rudolph et al., 2023).

The development of Agentic AI models that can engage in reliable academic literature review requires a multidisciplinary approach, incorporating insights from fields such as computer science, education, and social science (Dwivedi et al., 2019). Furthermore, it is essential to consider the ethical implications of AI adoption in academic settings, including issues related to bias, fairness, and transparency (Barredo Arrieta et al., 2019). By addressing these challenges and developing more advanced AI models, it is possible to create more reliable and effective academic literature review systems that can support researchers and educators in their work.
